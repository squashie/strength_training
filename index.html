Week 7

Progressive overload: On main lifts (squat, deadlift, press), aim to add 2.5–5 kg when you restart the cycle. On accessories, add reps first — once you hit the top of the rep range for all sets, add weight next cycle.

Fat loss tip: Keep rest periods 60–90s on accessory work (full 2–3 min on main lifts). This elevates heart rate and adds a conditioning effect. The biggest lever for fat loss remains diet — a modest calorie deficit of 300–500 kcal/day combined with this program is the sweet spot for losing fat while maintaining/building strength.
Weeks 1–2
Straight sets — establish baseline
Focus on form and finding working weights. Rest 90–120s between sets. Don't push to failure — leave 2–3 reps in the tank.
Weeks 3–4
Rep escalation — more volume
Add 1–2 reps per set vs week 1–2. Same or slightly heavier load. Rest 60–90s to increase metabolic demand.
Weeks 5–6
Load progression — go heavier
Drop reps back to week 1 levels but add weight (2.5–5 kg). This is your strength phase. Rest 2–3 min on main lifts.
Week 7
Deload — recovery week
Drop all weights by ~40%. Same exercises, max 3 sets. Focus on perfect technique. Let your body consolidate gains before restarting the cycle.
